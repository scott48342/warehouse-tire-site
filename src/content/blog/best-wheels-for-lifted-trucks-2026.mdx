# Best Wheels for Lifted Trucks in 2026: Complete Guide

If you've invested in lifting your truck, the next step is finding wheels that match your style and perform off-road. The right wheels can transform your lifted truck from stock-looking to head-turning.

## What to Consider When Choosing Lifted Truck Wheels

### 1. Wheel Size and Offset

For lifted trucks, you have more flexibility with wheel size. Most lifted trucks run **17-22 inch wheels**, with 20" being the sweet spot for balancing looks and tire selection.

**Offset matters.** A negative offset (like -12mm or -18mm) pushes the wheel outward, giving that aggressive stance. Just make sure your fenders can handle it.

### 2. Wheel Width

Wider wheels (9-12 inches) give your truck a more aggressive look and can accommodate wider tires. A popular setup is:
- **17x9** for a clean, functional look
- **20x10** for a balance of style and capability
- **22x12** for maximum aggression

### 3. Bolt Pattern Compatibility

Make sure your wheels match your truck's bolt pattern:
- **Ford F-150 (2015+):** 6x135
- **Chevy Silverado/GMC Sierra:** 6x139.7 (6x5.5)
- **RAM 1500:** 5x139.7 (5x5.5)
- **Toyota Tundra:** 5x150
- **Jeep Wrangler JL:** 5x127 (5x5)

## Recommended Setups by Truck

### Ford F-150 with 4-6" Lift
- **Wheels:** 20x10 with -18 offset
- **Tires:** 35x12.50R20
- **Look:** Aggressive but still functional

### Chevy Silverado with 6" Lift
- **Wheels:** 22x12 with -44 offset
- **Tires:** 33x12.50R22 or 35x12.50R22
- **Look:** Show truck stance

### RAM 1500 with 4" Lift
- **Wheels:** 20x9 with -12 offset
- **Tires:** 35x12.50R20
- **Look:** Balanced and practical

### Jeep Wrangler with 3-4" Lift
- **Wheels:** 17x9 with -12 offset
- **Tires:** 37x12.50R17
- **Look:** Trail-ready

## Installation Tips

1. **Get an alignment** after installing new wheels and tires
2. **Check for rubbing** at full lock and over bumps
3. **Consider wheel spacers** if you need more clearance
4. **Torque to spec** - lifted trucks put more stress on lug nuts
