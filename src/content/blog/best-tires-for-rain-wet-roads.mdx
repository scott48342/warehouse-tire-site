# Best Tires for Rain and Wet Roads in 2026

Wet roads cause over **1 million accidents per year** in the United States. The right tires can mean the difference between stopping safely and hydroplaning through an intersection.

If you live somewhere with frequent rain — the Pacific Northwest, Florida, or anywhere in the Southeast — wet traction should be your top tire priority.

## Why Wet Performance Matters

On dry pavement, most tires perform similarly. But add water, and differences become dramatic:

- **Stopping distance can vary by 20+ feet** between tires
- Poor tires can lose grip completely at just 35 mph in standing water
- Worn tires perform exponentially worse than new ones in rain

## What Makes a Tire Good in Rain?

### 1. Tread Design

**Circumferential grooves** (the channels running around the tire) evacuate water from the contact patch. More and deeper grooves = better water evacuation.

**Sipes** (tiny slits in the tread blocks) create additional biting edges that grip wet pavement.

### 2. Rubber Compound

**Silica-enhanced compounds** maintain flexibility in wet conditions, improving grip. Premium tires use advanced silica formulations that outperform basic rubber.

### 3. Tread Depth

New tires have 10/32" to 11/32" of tread. Once you reach 4/32", wet performance drops dramatically. At 2/32" (the legal minimum), your tires have lost most of their rain capability.

## How to Drive Safely in Rain

Even the best tires have limits. Follow these wet weather tips:

### Reduce Speed
Hydroplaning risk increases with speed. If roads are wet, slow down 5-10 mph below the limit.

### Increase Following Distance
Wet braking distances are longer. Keep at least 4 seconds of distance behind the car ahead.

### Avoid Puddles and Standing Water
Even great tires can hydroplane in deep standing water. Avoid puddles when possible.

### Smooth Inputs
Brake gently, accelerate gradually, and steer smoothly. Sudden movements can break traction.

### Check Your Tread
Insert a quarter into your tread, Washington's head down. If you see the top of his head, your tires need replacing.

## Warning Signs Your Tires Aren't Safe for Rain

Replace your tires immediately if you notice:

- **Visible wear bars** — Raised bars between tread blocks indicate minimum tread
- **Hydroplaning at moderate speeds** — If you lose grip below 45 mph, tires are worn
- **Longer stopping distances** — Takes noticeably longer to stop in rain
- **Cupping or uneven wear** — Creates unpredictable wet handling
- **Cracking or dry rot** — Compromises structural integrity
