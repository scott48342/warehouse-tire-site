# Best All-Terrain Tires for Trucks in 2026

If you're looking for tires that can handle your daily commute *and* weekend adventures, all-terrain tires are the perfect middle ground. These versatile tires deliver confident performance on highways while providing the grip you need when the pavement ends.

Whether you drive an F-150, Silverado, RAM 1500, or any other truck, choosing the right all-terrain tire can transform your driving experience. In this guide, we'll break down the best AT tires for 2026 and help you find the perfect match for your truck.

## What Makes a Great All-Terrain Tire?

Before diving into our top picks, let's understand what separates good all-terrain tires from great ones:

**On-Road Performance**
- Quiet, comfortable ride on highways
- Good handling in wet and dry conditions
- Even tread wear for long life

**Off-Road Capability**
- Aggressive tread pattern for mud and loose surfaces
- Reinforced sidewalls for rock protection
- Self-cleaning tread design

**Durability**
- Cut and chip resistant compound
- Strong internal construction
- Long treadwear warranty

The best all-terrain tires strike a balance between these factors without compromising too heavily in any area.

## How to Choose the Right All-Terrain Tire

### Consider Your Driving Habits

**Mostly Highway (80%+ on-road):** Choose a tire with a less aggressive tread pattern for better fuel economy and quieter ride. The Cooper Discoverer AT3 4S or Falken Wildpeak AT3W are excellent choices.

**Mixed Use (50/50):** The BFGoodrich KO2 or Toyo Open Country AT III offer the best balance for drivers who split time between pavement and trails.

**Mostly Off-Road (80%+ off-road):** Consider the Nitto Ridge Grappler or step up to a mud-terrain tire for maximum capability.

### Check Your Size

All-terrain tires come in a wide range of sizes to fit everything from stock trucks to lifted rigs. Common sizes include:

- **Stock trucks:** 265/70R17, 275/65R18, 275/60R20
- **Leveled trucks:** 275/70R17, 285/65R18, 285/55R20
- **Lifted trucks:** 285/70R17, 295/70R18, 35x12.50R20

Not sure what size fits your truck? [Use our fitment tool](/tires) to find compatible sizes for your year, make, and model.

### Think About Treadwear

All-terrain tires generally last 40,000-60,000 miles depending on driving habits and tire choice. If longevity is important, look for tires with longer treadwear warranties and harder compound designs.

## Installation Tips

Once you've chosen your all-terrain tires, proper installation ensures the best performance:

1. **Balance all four tires** — All-terrain tires can be heavier, making balance critical for a smooth ride
2. **Check your alignment** — Especially important if you've lifted your truck
3. **Set proper tire pressure** — Check your door jamb placard or owner's manual
4. **Rotate every 5,000-7,500 miles** — Maintains even wear and maximizes tire life

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are all-terrain tires good for daily driving?

Yes! Modern all-terrain tires are designed to be comfortable daily drivers. While they may be slightly louder than highway tires, the difference is minimal with quality brands like BFGoodrich and Falken.

### Do all-terrain tires hurt fuel economy?

All-terrain tires typically reduce fuel economy by 1-3 MPG compared to highway tires due to increased rolling resistance. However, many newer models have been optimized to minimize this impact.

### Can I use all-terrain tires in snow?

Many all-terrain tires carry the 3-Peak Mountain Snowflake (3PMSF) rating, meaning they meet minimum snow traction standards. They're suitable for light to moderate snow conditions but may not match dedicated winter tires in severe conditions.

### How long do all-terrain tires last?

Most quality all-terrain tires last 40,000-60,000 miles with proper maintenance. Factors like driving style, road conditions, and tire pressure maintenance affect longevity.

### Should I get load range E tires?

Load range E (10-ply) tires offer extra durability and load capacity but ride firmer than load range C or D. They're recommended for heavy trucks, frequent towing, or serious off-road use.
